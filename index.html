Key Findings

Data-Driven Story

Synthesising the visualisations above, several critical narratives emerge about Australian road safety enforcement in 2023.

The Speeding Epidemic

Speeding fines dominate every jurisdiction and age group, comprising 82% of all penalties. The 40-64 age group alone contributes $11.7M in speed fines and more than all other offence types combined across all ages.

However, arrests for speeding are minimal (228 total), suggesting enforcement relies heavily on automated detection rather than physical interception.

The Unlicensed Driving Paradox

Unlicensed driving generates relatively few fines ($44K) but drives 7,653 arrests / 83% of all arrests. This inverse relationship (low fines, high arrests) indicates unlicensed driving is treated as a serious criminal matter rather than a revenue-raising offence.

The 26-39 age group has the highest unlicensed arrest count (3,876), possibly reflecting licence suspensions from prior offences.

Urban vs Rural Divide

Major Cities account for 65% of all fines, but per-capita enforcement intensity is higher in regional areas when adjusted for population. Remote Australia shows disproportionately high arrest rates relative to fines.

Very Remote Australia has minimal absolute numbers but the highest ratio of arrests to fines, suggesting limited policing resources are focused on serious offences only.

Data Quality & Transparency

Significant gaps exist in the dataset: NSW, QLD, and VIC report zero arrests, which contradicts known policing activity.
